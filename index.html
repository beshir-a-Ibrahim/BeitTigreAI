üìö Overview

The Tigre language, primarily spoken in Eritrea and eastern regions of Sudan, is widely recognized as the closest living relative of Classical Geez‚Äîa historically significant Semitic language. Tigre remains a vibrant, living language with an expanding digital presence, increasingly represented across modern media platforms listed in the Reference section below and others.

Despite this vitality, Tigre has remained significantly underrepresented in the field of Natural Language Processing (NLP). This work aims to bridge that gap by introducing a comprehensive set of open resources and tools to support NLP research and development for the language. Tigre is a vital linguistic link to Classical Geez, which has limited accessible learning resources. Therefore, advancements in Tigre natural language processing (NLP) can offer valuable insights into Geez, facilitating its computational comprehension and potential revival.

We gratefully acknowledge the Tigre-speaking community for their enduring commitment to preserving and promoting their linguistic heritage. Their contributions‚Äîespecially through voice recordings, text corpora, and translation efforts‚Äîhave been instrumental in the data collection that underpins this work. Leveraging these community-driven resources, we have developed a suite of NLP models for Tigre, including:

A Large Language Model (LLM) (Llama 3.2-based, in progress)

Machine Translation systems

Automatic Speech Recognition (ASR)

Text-to-Speech (TTS) synthesis

fastText vector representations for both Tigre and Geez

These efforts reflect broader recognition of the language‚Äôs importance: Tigre and Geez are now included in the Meta/UNESCO Language Technology Partner Program, and Tigre is currently in the process of being integrated into Google Translate‚Äîmarking significant milestones in the digital inclusion of both languages.
