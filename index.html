üìö Overview

The Tigre language, primarily spoken in Eritrea and eastern regions of Sudan, is widely recognized as the closest living relative of Ge Ωez ‚Äî a historically significant Semitic language. Tigre remains a vibrant, living language with an expanding digital presence, increasingly represented across modern media platforms listed in the Reference section below.

Despite this vitality, Tigre has remained significantly underrepresented in the field of Natural Language Processing (NLP). This work aims to bridge that gap by introducing a comprehensive set of open resources and tools to support NLP research and development for the language.

The growth of Tigre NLP can also create opportunities to expand Ge Ωez NLP. Since Tigre is the closest living relative to Ge Ωez, the datasets, models, and tools developed for Tigre can be adapted or used as a foundation for Ge Ωez. This is particularly valuable given the limited resources available for Ge Ωez, a historically significant but no longer widely spoken language.

We gratefully acknowledge the Tigre-speaking community for their enduring commitment to preserving and promoting their linguistic heritage. Their contributions‚Äîespecially through voice recordings, text corpora, and translation efforts‚Äîhave been instrumental in the data collection that underpins this work.
