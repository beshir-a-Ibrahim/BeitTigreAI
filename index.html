üìö Overview

The Tigre language, primarily spoken in Eritrea and eastern Sudan, is recognized by scholars as the closest living relative to the ancient Geez language. While Geez is no longer spoken, Tigre remains a vibrant, living language with a growing presence in modern media. This includes a robust body of content across platforms such as Wikipedia, Commonvoice, tatoeba, newspapers, books, and broadcast media like radio and television.

The active engagement of the Tigre-speaking community in preserving and promoting their linguistic heritage was instrumental in the data collection efforts.

Despite its vitality, Tigre has received limited attention in the field of natural language processing (NLP). The materials presented in this work aim to address this gap and support the development of NLP resources for the language. Furthermore, given the scarcity of learning resources for Geez, Tigre offers a unique linguistic bridge. Its study can provide valuable insights for understanding and potentially revitalizing its classical counterpart.

Leveraging the collected data, we have developed a suite of NLP models for Tigre, including a large language model (LLM), machine translation, automatic speech recognition (ASR), and text-to-speech (TTS), in addition to a fastText vector representation for both Tigre and Geez
