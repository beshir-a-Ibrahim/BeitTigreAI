üìö Overview

Tigre is a Semitic language spoken by a significant portion of Eritrea‚Äôs population. It serves as the mother tongue of the native Tigre people and as a second language for several other ethnic groups, making it a vital means of communication across diverse communities in Eritrea. Scholars recognize Tigre as one of the oldest Semitic languages in the region, preserving ancient linguistic features that offer valuable insights into the history and development of Semitic languages.

Despite its historical and cultural significance, Tigre remains significantly underrepresented in the field of Natural Language Processing (NLP). This project aims to bridge that gap by introducing a comprehensive set of open resources and tools to support NLP research and development for the language. Furthermore, advancements in Tigre NLP could pave the way for similar progress in Ge Ωez, its closest relative and a historically significant Semitic language.

Both Tigre and Ge Ωez have recently been integrated into the Meta/UNESCO Language Technology Partner Program and Google Translate, marking major steps forward in their digital recognition and accessibility. This work, built upon community-driven data, contributes to these ongoing efforts by developing a suite of NLP artifacts to advance the digital presence of these languages.
